## 2026-07-15 RAIN JOINS WATER BODIES (same session, user: "make rain pool into water and add to bodies of water")

- **Absorption rule** (`Cells.JoinsWaterBody`, checked in TickLiquid before the evaporation roll): a RAIN-tagged cell touching untagged (permanent) water on any cardinal side — or buried under a FULL TILE (Density cells) of continuous water — sheds its tag (`_srcTile=0`) + WakeNeighbors so the untag spreads through the connected puddle by the same contact rule. Showers permanently raise lakes/seas; a basin filling ≥1 tile deep becomes a real pond from the bottom up. **Depth bar is deliberate**: first cut used "any sandwiched cell" (3 cells = 1.5px) — that would turn every dip permanent and kill the evaporation sink (the creeping flood the tag exists to prevent).
- **`DripWaterSrc = 254`**: ceiling-drip water re-tagged (was RainWaterSrc) — evaporates like rain but NEVER converts. Drips mass-duplicate from the pool above by design; an absorbing drip = unbounded cave flooding under every lake. Snow-melt water stays RainWaterSrc (bounded by the snow spawn cap, may join lakes).
- SimTest `TestRainPooling`: shower over an obsidian pool pocket → count rises permanently after 20s calm (3263→3364); identical DRY pocket at another angle → thin film dries to ~1 cell. TRAP: first control build was a floating sky-band obsidian "tray" (surfaceR+8, rim on a different-tile-count ring) — rain never stayed in it, 28/30 "survived" and looked like an evaporation bug; carve test fixtures as GROUND pockets by angle, not sky shelves.
